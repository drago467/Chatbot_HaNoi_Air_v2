### Mechanism Explanation
From Introduction:
- "Forests are effective for absorbing and filtering PM"
- "The forest canopy can increase PM dry deposition by leading air movement"
- "A study in England showed that the concentration of inhalable particles in the West Midlands was reduced by 10% and that 10 t PM10 settled every year as the urban forest coverage increased from 3.7% to 16.5%"
- "Fifty percent of PMs produced by the open cast mining process could be reduced by a 15 km wide birch forest"
- "In Mexico, the forests in media areas can remove 100 t PM10 from the air every year, amounting to 2% of annual emissions"

### Factors Affecting Deposition
- Canopy density and plant species
- Relatively sparse forests more efficient in PM deposition
- Meteorological factors: wind speed, humidity, temperature
- Shorter tree-building distance and canopy-canopy distance can reduce PM concentrations more efficiently

### Conclusions from Paper
1. Forests, wetlands and lakes can deposit PMs efficiently while forest has best ability to remove fine particle pollution
2. Maximum fine particle deposition occurred in winter
3. Dry deposition influenced by both atmospheric condition and PM concentrations
