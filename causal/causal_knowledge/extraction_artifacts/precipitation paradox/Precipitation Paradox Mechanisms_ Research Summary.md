# Precipitation Paradox Mechanisms: Research Summary

## Overview

This research investigates the **Precipitation Paradox** phenomenon, where precipitation events (particularly light rain, drizzle, and fog) do not reduce or may even increase PM2.5 concentrations. This counterintuitive behavior challenges the conventional understanding that rainfall always improves air quality through wet scavenging.

## Key Mechanisms Identified

### 1. Light Rain Paradox (Drizzle Effect)

The most significant finding is that drizzle (precipitation intensity < 2 mm/h) can exacerbate PM2.5 pollution under cleaner baseline conditions (≤ 35 µg/m³). This occurs because:

- Light precipitation increases relative humidity (RH > 85%) without providing sufficient wet scavenging force
- High humidity promotes aqueous-phase reactions that form secondary organic aerosols (SOA)
- Lower pH conditions during drizzle catalyze acid-catalyzed SOA formation
- The effect is most pronounced during spring season

**Quantitative Evidence**: PM2.5 and its constituents (notably NO₃⁻) significantly increased after daytime drizzle and light rain events in Chengdu, China [1].

### 2. Aerosol Liquid Water (ALW) Enhancement

Aerosol liquid water content (ALWC) plays a critical role in the paradox mechanism:

- ALWC contributed **66.68%** to PM2.5 mass when RH > 85%
- A self-amplifying feedback loop exists: ALW increases → SIA formation increases → ALW increases further
- The sensitivity is higher for nitrate (R = 0.94) than sulfate (R = 0.90)

**RH Thresholds**:
| Component | RH Threshold | Effect |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Sulfate | > 50% | Aqueous-phase oxidation begins |
| Nitrate | > 80% | Gas-particle partitioning favors particle phase |
| Mass growth | > 85% | Exponential increase in growth factor |

### 3. Fog Processing and Post-Fog Enhancement

Fog events demonstrate a dual effect:

**During fog**: PM2.5 can be scavenged (reduced from 150 to 45 µg/m³, ~70% reduction)

**After fog dissipation**: PM2.5 increases significantly due to release of processed aerosols:
- Sulfate: +23%
- Nitrate: +24%
- Ammonium: +14%
- Total PM2.5: +17%

### 4. N₂O₅ Heterogeneous Chemistry

The mutual promotion between ALW and particulate nitrate creates a rapid air quality degradation mechanism:

- N₂O₅ uptake coefficient enhanced by **factor of 9** (from 0.002 to 0.018)
- Loss rate (k_N₂O₅) increased by **factor of 25** when accounting for surface area increase
- This mechanism can halve visibility within 1 day

### 5. Precipitation Intensity Thresholds

Wet scavenging efficiency depends non-linearly on rainfall rate:

| Aerosol Mode | Optimal Scavenging Rate (Tropics) |
|--------------|-----------------------------------|
| Aitken | 10-12 mm/d |
| Accumulation | 8-9 mm/d |
| Coarse | 7-8 mm/d |

**Key insight**: Precipitation frequency is more important than intensity for aerosol wet removal.

## Relevance to Southeast Asia and Vietnam

The research identified specific relevance to the Hanoi and Southeast Asian context:

1. **Monsoon Influence**: Aerosol acidity in Hanoi is closely related to monsoon air masses
2. **Regional Transport**: Northeast monsoon brings acidic aerosols from China; Southwest monsoon brings alkaline aerosols from Thailand/Cambodia
3. **High Humidity Conditions**: Prolonged high RH during monsoon seasons creates favorable conditions for aqueous-phase chemistry
