# Paper 3: Wang et al. 2020 - Fast Sulfate Formation in Beijing Haze

## Citation
- **Title**: Fast sulfate formation from oxidation of SO2 by NO2 and HONO observed in Beijing haze
- **Authors**: Junfeng Wang, Jingyi Li, Jianhuai Ye, Jian Zhao, et al.
- **Year**: 2020
- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41467-020-16683-x
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-020-16683-x
- **Tier**: Tier-1 (Peer-reviewed journal - Nature Communications)

## Key Findings

### 1. Fog/Cloud Processing Evidence
The paper shows evidence that sulfate shifts to larger particle sizes during haze events, indicative of fog/cloud processing. Fog and cloud readily form under winter haze conditions, leading to high liquid water contents with high pH (>5.5) from elevated ammonia.

### 2. Two-Step Fog-Enabled Mechanism
The study proposes a two-step fog-enabled mechanism for sulfate formation:
1. Aqueous-phase oxidation of SO2 by NO2, producing HONO
2. HONO can in turn oxidize SO2 to yield N2O

### 3. Stage I vs Stage II Conditions
- **Stage I** (moderate RH 40-75%): PM2.5 ~210 μg/m³, sulfate ~10 μg/m³
- **Stage II** (high RH >75%): PM2.5 ~330 μg/m³, sulfate ~40 μg/m³ (fourfold increase)
- Dense nighttime fog with LWC as high as 0.5 g/m³ observed

### 4. Key Observations
- SO2 concentrations were relatively high in Stage I but nearly depleted in Stage II, indicating rapid oxidation
- Sulfate particles shifted to larger sizes during Stage II while organic particles did not
- N2O concentrations rose concurrently with sulfate (evidence for HONO pathway)

### 5. Key Quotes
> "Severe events of wintertime particulate air pollution in Beijing (winter haze) are associated with high relative humidity (RH) and fast production of particulate sulfate from the oxidation of sulfur dioxide (SO2) emitted by coal combustion."

> "Here we show evidence from field observations of a haze event that rapid oxidation of SO2 by nitrogen dioxide (NO2) and nitrous acid (HONO) takes place."

> "Sulfate shifts to larger particle sizes during the event, indicative of fog/cloud processing."

> "Fog and cloud readily form under winter haze conditions, leading to high liquid water contents with high pH (>5.5) from elevated ammonia."

> "Such conditions enable fast aqueous-phase oxidation of SO2 by NO2, producing HONO which can in turn oxidize SO2 to yield N2O."

## Relevance to Edge Cases
This paper is highly relevant to edge cases because:
1. It explains the "non-intuitive" rapid sulfate formation during fog/haze events
2. Shows that fog processing is a key mechanism for accelerated chemistry
3. Demonstrates that high RH + fog → rapid sulfate formation (not just measurement artifact)
