## Key Findings

### Mechanism: UV Photolysis → OH Radical Formation

This paper demonstrates that OH radicals are formed by photolysis of secondary organic aerosol (SOA) material formed by terpene ozonolysis. The key findings include:

1. **Photolysis of SOA produces OH radicals**: UV light exposure of SOA material generates hydroxyl radicals through photolysis of organic hydroperoxides (ROOH) and H2O2 present in the aerosol.

2. **OH formation rates from SOA are 5 times faster** than from the photolysis of H2O2 solutions alone, indicating that organic hydroperoxides (ROOH) are more efficient OH sources.

3. **Quantum yield for OH generation**: The quantum yield for OH generation from α-pinene SOA is 0.8 ± 0.4, which is remarkably high.

4. **Implications**: This is the first demonstration of an efficient photolytic source of OH in SOA, one that may affect both cloud water and aerosol chemistry.
