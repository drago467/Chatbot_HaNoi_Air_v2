# Paper Notes: Tie et al. 2003 - Effect of Clouds on Photolysis and Oxidants

## Citation
- **Title**: Effect of clouds on photolysis and oxidants in the troposphere
- **Authors**: Xuexi Tie, Sasha Madronich, Stacy Walters, Renyi Zhang, Phil Rasch, William Collins
- **Year**: 2003
- **Journal**: Journal of Geophysical Research: Atmospheres
- **DOI**: https://doi.org/10.1029/2003JD003659
- **Tier**: Tier 1 (JGR - peer-reviewed)

## Key Findings

### Cloud Effects on Photolysis Rates
The study demonstrates that clouds have important impacts on tropospheric chemistry through their effects on photolysis rates (J values).

**Quantitative Effects:**
- Global mean OH concentration increases by **~20%** due to cloud impact
- J[O3] enhanced by **~12%** in troposphere due to clouds
- J[CH2O] enhanced by **~13%** due to clouds
- J[NO2] enhanced by **~13%** due to clouds
- Tropospheric O3 concentrations increase by **~8%** due to cloud effects

### Mechanism
Cloud layers influence photolysis rates through:
1. Back scattering on top of clouds
2. Enhancement of UV radiation through scattering
3. Modification of actinic flux distribution

## Relevant Quotes
> "Global mean OH concentration increases by about 20% due to the impact of clouds."

> "Because of clouds, the globally averaged photolysis rates of J[O3], J[CH2O], and J[NO2] are enhanced in the troposphere by about 12, 13, and 13%, respectively, leading to an 8% increase in the tropospheric O3 concentrations."

> "Clouds strongly influence photolysis rates and hence play an important role in controlling the concentrations of the tropospheric oxidants."

## Applicability to Hanoi/Vietnam

- Important for modeling SOA formation under varying cloud conditions
