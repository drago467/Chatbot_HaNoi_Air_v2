# Paper Notes: Zhang et al. 2018 - Monoterpene SOA Formation

## Citation
- **Title**: Monoterpenes are the largest source of summertime organic aerosol in the southeastern United States
- **Authors**: Haofei Zhang, Lindsay D Yee, Ben H Lee, et al.
- **Year**: 2018
- **Journal**: Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS)
- **DOI**: https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1717513115
- **Tier**: Tier 1 (PNAS - peer-reviewed, highly cited)

## Key Findings

### Monoterpene SOA Contribution
The study provides comprehensive molecular characterization of atmospheric organic aerosol and establishes monoterpenes as the dominant source of summertime SOA.

**Quantitative Results:**
- Monoterpene SOA accounts for **approximately 50%** of total fine organic aerosol (OA) in summertime
- Isoprene SOA + biomass burning OA together account for ~30% of total fine OA
- Particle-phase organic nitrates (pONs) from NO3 oxidation of BVOC: 5-12% of fine OA

### Formation Mechanisms
1. **Daytime oxidation**: OH radical oxidation of monoterpenes
2. **Nighttime oxidation**: NO3 radical oxidation of monoterpenes
3. **Ozonolysis**: O3 + monoterpenes → SOA

**Key Monoterpene Precursors:**
- α-pinene
- Limonene
- Myrcene

### NOx Influence
The study demonstrates strong anthropogenic-biogenic interaction:
> "Monoterpene secondary organic aerosol mass increases with enhanced nitrogen oxide processing, indicating anthropogenic influence on biogenic secondary organic aerosol formation."

### Oxidation Products
Known MTSOA tracers include:
- Pinonic acid (C10H16O3)
- Pinic acid (C9H14O4)
- 3-hydroxyglutaric acid (C5H8O5)
- MBTCA (C8H12O6)

## Relevant Quotes
> "Secondary OA (SOA) from monoterpene oxidation accounts for approximately half of summertime fine OA in Centreville, AL, a forested area in the southeastern United States influenced by anthropogenic pollution."

> "Different chemical processes involving nitrogen oxides, during days and nights, play a central role in determining the mass of monoterpene SOA produced."

## Applicability to Hanoi/Vietnam
- Southeast Asia has significant monoterpene emissions from tropical vegetation
- Anthropogenic-biogenic interactions relevant for urban-rural interface
- NOx influence on biogenic SOA formation applicable to polluted tropical regions
